## Summary (from the public teaser)

Aakash Gupta — who reports coaching 200+ candidates and helping 30+ land AI PM roles — argues the AI PM interview has changed structurally between 2023 and 2026, and frames the guide around five shifts:

1. **Built, not just used** — interviews now probe whether you've actually *built* AI features (architecture, metrics, business impact), not just used AI tools.
2. **"Vibe coding" rounds** — a live build round using tools like Cursor or Bolt is now real.
3. **AI product sense** has largely replaced traditional product-sense evaluation.
4. **AI-specific behavioral depth** — generic STAR-method stories no longer suffice.
5. **Safety is woven throughout** the loop rather than isolated to a single round.

The public section name-checks the companies hiring for these roles (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Meta, Amazon, plus the likes of Perplexity, Stripe, Figma, Nvidia) and includes a mock-interview reference, while the detailed question banks and tools sit behind the paywall.
